About

Why Basketball?

Basketball is the most democratic sport in the world. All you need is a ball and a hoop — and suddenly a parking lot becomes Madison Square Garden. I've been playing since I was eight years old, and the game has taught me more about teamwork, resilience, and discipline than anything else in my life.

There's also something uniquely beautiful about the geometry of a perfectly executed play — the skip pass that opens a corner three, the pick-and-roll that collapses the defense, the moment when five players move as one organism.

The Basics

Core Fundamentals

Every great player, no matter how flashy, builds their game on these foundations. Here's how they rank in terms of what coaches emphasize first:

  1. Defense
    • Defensive stance — stay low, feet shoulder-width
    • Help defense — always be aware of the ball
    • Closeouts — sprint, then slow down before contact
    • Rebounding position — box out before the ball drops
  2. Ball Handling
    • Dribble with fingertips, not the palm
    • Keep your eyes up — see the whole floor
    • Change of pace and direction over raw speed
  3. Shooting
    • BEEF: Balance, Eyes, Elbow, Follow-through
    • Catch and shoot vs. off-the-dribble mechanics differ
    • Repetition builds muscle memory — 500 makes per day
  4. Passing
    • Pass to where your teammate is going, not where they are
    • Chest pass for most situations; bounce pass in traffic
  5. Footwork
    • Pivoting to protect the ball under pressure
    • Jab steps and shot fakes to create space
All-Time

The Greats

Every generation has its debates. But a few names transcend argument. Here are the players who changed the game forever:

Michael Jordan

6× NBA champion, 5× MVP. Defined the modern wing scorer. The standard everyone is measured against.

LeBron James

4× champion, 4× MVP. The most complete player ever — elite scorer, passer, rebounder, and defender.

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

All-time leading scorer (for decades). The skyhook is the most unguardable shot in NBA history.

Magic Johnson

Revolutionized the point guard position at 6'9". Made everyone around him better. 5× champion.

Bill Russell

11 championships in 13 years. The greatest winner in American team sports, full stop.

Steph Curry

Changed the geometry of basketball. Made the 30-foot three-pointer a viable primary weapon.
